The woman behind the heel

Lee's journey:
from R3000 to two stores

R3000
The loan that started an empire

In 2020, a friend handed Lee R3000. "Start something," she said. That something became Lee Luxe.

Lee sat in a Sandton call centre, headset on, taking call after call. She was good at it — the clients loved her voice, her patience. But something was missing. She dreamed of buildings, of architecture, of creating things that stood tall.

One Friday afternoon, she resigned. No backup plan. Just a feeling that she was meant for more.

"Months of nothing. Rent due. Calls ignored. The silence was loud."

Then a friend, someone who had watched her struggle, handed her an envelope. Inside: R3000 in cash. "Start something," she said. Lee bought three pairs of stilettos from a small manufacturer, photographed them on her kitchen floor, and posted them on Instagram.

The first pair sold within an hour. Then the second. By the end of the week, all three were gone. She reinvested everything. The side hustle became a business. The business became a brand.

Today, Lee Luxe has two stores — one in Hatfield, one in Sandton — and a growing online family. Every heel carries that original fire: the belief that one woman, with R3000 and a dream, can build something lasting.
